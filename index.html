What?

Getting New York visitors to instantly know manhattan’s north, south, west, and east! Which makes the daily-walk of a native New Yorker much smoother!

Why?

It happens often, especially at the intersections, when tourists trying to figure out "from where to where", obstructing native New Yorkers during their rush-walk to the subway!

How?

According to the federal laws, adding icons on traffic signs are mostly prohibited, and to get the FHWA approval to add letters as we're seeking to do so! it must be the latest FHWA formal font, which known as Clearview Hwy Font.
New York State uses national MUTCD with state supplement, but for our case of the one-way traffic sign aka R6-1, and after revisioning the state supplement Section 2B.40, NYC DOT & NYS DOT still restrictive to the font requirement.

How did we protect this idea?

We took advantage of the regulation's gap which requires a specific font, and we've patent design the one-way sign [R6-1] with cardinal directions letters positioned in different areas of the sign itself, and surely we used the exclusive federal font - aka Clearview Hwy, which owned by a Foundry called Terminal Design Inc.

Since the foundry owns the font, not the use of the font! We've embraced our opportunity to protect the use of letters for guiding tourists exclusively in New York City, which means anyone else with the same idea using a different font, it won't match the federal regulations, only our intellectual properties were invented to do so! We're also aiming to partner with NY city/state authorities and NYer tourism advocacies to make it happen, for the love of native New Yorkers who're welcoming visitors but they need to walk seamlessly as well!
